Pace Plane — improving the read

Three things to fix: (1) axis labels are missing — they should just say TIME and USAGE. (2) a dot below the even-burn diagonal means overspending (used more than the time gone) and above means surplus — that sign should be obvious at a glance, not inferred. (3) windows cap each other: 100k left this week but 200k left in the 5-hour slot means you really only have 100k before a wall — that binding limit needs to be visible. Below: a few directions for each, on one shared sample.

5-hour window20k / 220k · 20% time
weekly window900k / 1.0M · 60% time
5h remaining200k resets 4h
weekly remaining100k resets 4d

The 5-hour slot is barely touched (well above the diagonal — a surplus); the weekly is burning hot (far below the diagonal — overspent). They make a clean over/under pair to test every idea against.

Part 1 · Axis labels + the over/under signal

Same plot in every card — usage grows left→right, time grows bottom→top, the dashed diagonal is a perfectly even burn. Each card adds the sign a different way. Dot fill stays the window's identity colour (so weekly vs 5-hour never gets ambiguous); the over/under signal is layered on top.

Part 2 · Limits capping each other

The plane normalises everything to 0..1, which is exactly why it cannot show that 100k < 200k — absolute headroom is lost.
